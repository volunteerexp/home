My ESC Experience

In Austria, I volunteered with the European Solidarity Corps at an after-school care center, where I helped with homework, organized activities, and supported the pedagogues. Initially stressed and inexperienced in living abroad, I sought change after two years in a pet store. My mum introduced me to ESC, and I was immediately interested, especially since my girlfriend would be studying in Vienna. The experience was transformative, as I learned to live independently and developed meaningful relationships with flatmates, other volunteers, and colleagues. Making a positive impact on the children's lives while experiencing personal growth was the highlight. I recommend ESC volunteering for personal and professional development, and advise future volunteers to embrace the experience, learn the local language, seek help when needed, and immerse in the culture. This year in Austria has been incredibly rewarding and memorable.
